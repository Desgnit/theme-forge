Lakes & prices

Three very different waters on one farm — pick your lake, pick your peg, and pay on the bank. Barbless hooks only on all three; the full rules are on the find-us page.

Willow Pool

Our original lake: 24 pegs around two and a half acres, carp to 28lb with a strong head of mid-doubles, plus quality tench as a bonus. Fishes well close in — you rarely need to cast far.

Kingfisher Pool

A friendly mixed coarse water stuffed with tench, bream and roach. Bites all day on float or feeder, which makes it perfect for youngsters, newcomers and anyone who just wants a bend in the rod.

Specimen Lake

Our big-fish water, home to carp to 35lb and low-stocked on purpose. Members and night syndicate only, with a waiting list — ask at the lodge or use the enquiry form to join it.

Tackle shop & bait

The lodge shop stocks bait, hooks, line and terminal tackle, with hot drinks all day and bacon rolls at weekends. Forgotten your landing net? We hire them for £2 a day.

Match bookings

Clubs can book Willow or Kingfisher for matches of up to 24 pegs, weekdays or weekends. Pegs roped off, results board at the lodge, and the kettle on when you weigh in.

Access & facilities

Hard paths from the car park, purpose-built disabled pegs on every lake, and clean toilets by the lodge. You can drop tackle at most pegs before parking up.

Tickets

Prices — pay at the lodge or on the bank

  1. £8

    Day ticket

    One rod, dawn till dusk, Willow or Kingfisher. Concessions £5 for juniors, over-65s and blue badge holders. Two rods £12.

  2. £5

    Evening ticket

    From 4pm till dusk on Willow or Kingfisher — ideal after work when the carp drift into the margins.

  3. £180

    Season ticket

    Twelve months on Willow and Kingfisher, two rods included, plus 10% off in the tackle shop. Junior season £90.

  4. £15

    Night fishing

    Specimen Lake only, per night, and only for night syndicate members. Syndicate membership £250 a year — waiting list applies.

After a place on Specimen Lake?

The syndicate waiting list is shorter than you'd think — ring the lodge and we'll talk you through how it works and when a peg is likely to come up.
