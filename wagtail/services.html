Services & prices

Every appointment starts with a consultation, and the price is agreed before any water goes on. Guide prices below by size: small (Yorkie, Shih Tzu), medium (cockapoo, cocker), large (Labrador, standard poodle) and giant (Newfoundland, Bernese).

Full groom

Bath with breed-appropriate shampoo, fluff dry, full clip or scissor styling to your dog's shape, nails, ears and a finishing spritz.

  • Small £38 · Medium £46
  • Large £58 · Giant from £75

Bath & tidy

Bath, blow dry, thorough brush-out, then face, feet, feathers and sanitary areas tidied — the in-between groom that keeps a coat manageable.

  • Small £28 · Medium £34
  • Large £42 · Giant from £55

Puppy introduction sessions

Two gentle half-hour visits for pups from ten weeks (fully vaccinated): explore the salon, hear the dryer, paws handled, endless treats. No clipping until they're ready.

  • First session £22, any size
  • Second session £12

Hand stripping

Traditional hand strip for wiry coats — border terriers, schnauzers, wire fox terriers — preserving the colour and harsh texture that clippers would soften and ruin.

  • Small £45 · Medium £58
  • Large £72 · Giant by consultation

De-shedding treatment

Double-coat soak, deep conditioner, high-velocity dry and a full rake-out for Labradors, huskies, shepherds and other champion moulters. The fluff stays with us.

  • Small £32 · Medium £40
  • Large £52 · Giant from £65

Nail trims & teeth

Walk-in nail trims between grooms, plus gentle enzymatic teeth cleaning as an add-on to any appointment. Nervous feet are handled slowly, one claw at a time.

  • Nail trim £10, any size
  • Teeth cleaning add-on £8

Guide prices assume a coat in reasonable condition. Tangles and matting add time, so they can add cost — we'll always agree the final price with you at the consultation, and we'll never shave a dog without talking to you first.

How it works

From hello to handover, in four steps

  1. 1

    Tell us about your dog

    Breed, size, coat condition and any quirks — the booking form asks everything we need.

  2. 2

    Meet & consult

    We check the coat together, agree the trim and confirm the price before any water goes on.

  3. 3

    The groom

    One dog in the building, no cages, breaks whenever the tail asks for one. Nervous dogs set the pace.

  4. 4

    Handover & photos

    A freshly gorgeous dog, a photo for the fridge, brushing tips and your next slot pencilled in.

Coat in a state? Don't be embarrassed.

Matted coats happen to the loveliest owners. We deal with them kindly, comfort first, and we'll show you how to keep on top of it afterwards. Call and tell us the worst — we've seen it.
