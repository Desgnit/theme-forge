Match report · WVL Division One

Longmead 187/6 beat Ashcombe 186/8: Fenwick holds his nerve in last-over chase

The Meadow · Sat 5 July 2026 · Att. 312

Report by Dele Adeyemi

Longmead 187/6 (39.2 ov) beat Ashcombe 186/8 (40 ov)

Harradine 68, Fenwick 41* · Chandran 3-31  |  Prentice 74 · Garside 3-38

If you left at tea, you got the quiet half. What followed was two and a half hours of the best kind of village cricket — a target that never felt safe, a chase that never felt lost, and a captain walking off at twenty past seven with 41 not out and a face like he'd done nothing more dramatic than mow the outfield.

Ashcombe set 187

The visitors won the toss, batted, and for a while looked like posting something rude. Prentice played beautifully for his 74, working Dahl into the gaps and pulling anything short — the two hundred and twenty that seemed likely at drinks would have been a different afternoon entirely.

Enter Priya Chandran. Her leg-spin has been the story of our season, and 3-31 here made it 41 wickets for the summer. The googly that did for Prentice, drawn down the pitch and stumped by yards, drew applause from both benches. Okoye's 2-29 up front and two run-outs in the last three overs kept Ashcombe to 186/8 — thirty short of what they wanted, twenty more than we'd have liked.

The wobble

Chases at The Meadow have a rhythm, and this one started off-beat. Garside bowled his eight straight through — quick, full, horrible — and had Fofana lbw, Hopkins caught behind and Bickford bowled through the gate. Ten overs in we were 34/3, the tea hut queue had gone quiet, and the rain radar was getting more attention than the scoreboard.

"Forty-over cricket at this level is simple: bat properly for thirty overs and madly for ten. We did it the other way round." — Danny Whitlow, keeping it honest

Harradine steadies it

The recovery was built the way Sam Harradine builds everything — no risks, no fuss, singles turned into twos by sheer bad manners. His 68 came off 81 balls and contained precisely three shots in anger, all of them boundaries, all of them off Garside's replacement. With Byrne busy at the other end, the hundred partnership came up in the 31st over and the impossible had quietly become arithmetic.

Then, of course, the wobble's sequel: Harradine top-edged a sweep in the 35th, Byrne holed out two overs later, and what should have been a stroll became seven needed off the last with four wickets in hand.

Seven off the last

Fenwick had been there since the 20th over, mostly in defence, entirely in charge. First ball of the 40th, Ashcombe's captain brought the field up; Fenwick hit the ball over it, one bounce into the sightscreen for four. Second ball, a clip off the hip to fine leg, three scampered — through for the win with four balls to spare, bat raised roughly one inch, because that's Fenwick. It leaves us second on 249 points, thirteen behind Nether Stowey with four rounds left — and we go to Church Field on 22 August. That one has been circled since June, and on this evidence, nobody at The Meadow fears it.

Scorecard

  • Ashcombe 186/8 (40 ov): Prentice 74, Whitham 31; Chandran 3-31, Okoye 2-29.
  • Longmead 187/6 (39.2 ov): Harradine 68, Fenwick 41*, Byrne 27; Garside 3-38.
  • Umpires: A. Pilkington & J. Draycott · Attendance: 312
