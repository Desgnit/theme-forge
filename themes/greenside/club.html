One hundred and nineteen summers

Founded by the men of the Avonford brickworks in 1907, still owned by its members, and still bowling on the same green their wages paid to lay.

Our story

Built by the town it plays for

Avonford Bowls Club was formed in the spring of 1907, when a group of brickworks men and the stationmaster clubbed together to rent the old orchard plot off Orchard Lane and lay a green by hand. The flat green they turfed that first summer is the one we still play on, and apart from a spell during the wars, bowls has been played there every season since.

The club is a members' association — no owner, no debt, every big decision voted on at the AGM over tea and fruit cake. Seven county pennants, five league titles and a crown green added in 1932 later, the point is the same as it was in 1907: a fine green, good company, and an afternoon well spent.

Orchard Lane

Two greens, one clubhouse

The flat green — six rinks, laid in 1907 and kept true by one very patient greenkeeper — hosts the league and county matches. Beside it sits the crown green, added in 1932 for friendlies and anyone who likes their bowls with a bit of contour. Between the two stands the 1928 timber clubhouse: honours boards, a proper tea urn, and the best veranda view in the county.

  • Visitors always welcome — green fees £5 a session, bowls loaned free
  • Free parking at the gate; the number 14 bus stops on Orchard Lane
  • Level paths all round both greens, benches every few yards, accessible toilets in the clubhouse
