Club championship finals day: Donnelly wins a seventh singles title at 71

The flat green, Orchard Lane · Sat 4 July 2026 · Att. 140

Report by Brian Tudor

Donnelly 21 beat Whitfield 18

Singles final, 21 shots up · Pairs: Ellison & Pryce 19–15 Hackett & Deakin · Triples: Tudor, Osman & Bramley 16–14 Farrar's three

If you only came for the cream teas, you still got your money's worth. Finals day at Orchard Lane gave us three finals, two measures, one shot decided by the width of Jean Hackett's thumbnail — and, at the end of it all, the sight of Ray Donnelly lifting the singles trophy for a seventh time, thirty-eight years after his first.

The pairs: decided by a millimetre

The morning belonged to the pairs. Margaret Ellison and Sylvia Pryce, county shield winners both, were expected to have too much class for Jean Hackett and Pam Deakin — and for twelve ends they didn't. Deakin's drawing was a revelation, and at 14–14 with two ends left, the clubhouse emptied onto the bank.

The seventeenth end needed the measure, and then a second opinion, and then the old brass callipers from the trophy cabinet. Shot to Ellison and Pryce, by a millimetre at most, and with it the momentum: a full house of four on the last gave them the title 19–15. Deakin was first across the green to shake hands, which tells you everything about her.

The triples: the quiet men have their day

The afternoon triples final was billed as a stroll for Colin Farrar's three, unbeaten in the competition. Nobody told Brian Tudor, Derek Osman and Ted Bramley — a secretary, a greenkeeper and a man who first picked up a wood three summers ago at the newcomers' roll-up. They led from the fourth end and held on 16–14, Bramley drawing shot on the last when anything loose would have handed it away.

"Three years ago Ted came to a taster session because his neighbour wouldn't stop going on about it. Today he's a club champion. That's the whole club in one sentence." — Margaret Ellison, President

The singles: Donnelly, of course

And so to the main event: Ray Donnelly against Gordon Whitfield, the same final as 2019, the same two men who have shared most of the club's silverware for a decade. Whitfield started the sharper and led 9–4, playing the runs and shots Donnelly's steadier game doesn't attempt. The veranda, which knows both men's games by heart, went unusually quiet.

What followed was a masterclass in not panicking. Donnelly drew his way back a shot at a time — never two woods in the ditch, never a wasted delivery — and levelled at 14–14 off an end that included three woods within a foot of the jack. At 18–18, three shots from the title each, Whitfield's final wood ran a yard through the head. Donnelly's reply pulled up six inches short of the jack, dead in line. Two more ends, three more shots, and at 21–18 the green rose to a 71-year-old champion who raised his cap roughly one inch, because that's Ray.

Results in brief

  • Singles final: R. Donnelly 21, G. Whitfield 18 — Donnelly's seventh title.
  • Pairs final: M. Ellison & S. Pryce 19, J. Hackett & P. Deakin 15 (18 ends).
  • Triples final: B. Tudor, D. Osman & T. Bramley 16, C. Farrar's three 14 (16 ends).
  • Markers: A. McAllister & H. Sohal · Attendance: 140, plus one very patient dog.
BT

Brian Tudor

Club secretary and newsletter editor since 2009. Lost the triples final he's reporting on here, and has been remarkably gracious about it.
