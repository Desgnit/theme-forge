The groomer behind the wagging tails

One groomer, one calm little salon in Hoole, and a van that brings the bubbles to your kerb.

Ellie's story

It started with a nervous spaniel called Bramble

Ellie qualified with her City & Guilds Level 3 in 2016 and spent two years in a busy high-street salon — six dogs in at once, dryers roaring, everyone slightly on edge. Her own spaniel, Bramble, hated every minute of it. So in 2018 she opened Wagtail to do the opposite: one dog in the building at a time, no cages, no queue of barking strangers, and appointments long enough that nobody has to be rushed.

The mobile van joined in 2021, for the dogs the salon doesn't suit — giant breeds, car-sick travellers, elderly dogs who'd rather not go anywhere. Heated water, a walk-up ramp, and the same unhurried groom on your own driveway. Bramble, now grey around the muzzle, still supervises quality control from her bed in the corner.

How we keep it calm

The calm salon philosophy

One dog at a time

Appointments never overlap, so your dog is the only dog in the building. No cages, no kennels, no waiting their turn next to a barking stranger.

Never rushed

Nervous dogs get extra time at no extra charge, breaks happen whenever the tail asks for one, and if today isn't the day, we'll finish another day rather than push.

Honest about coats

If a coat is too matted to save comfortably, we'll say so and clip it kindly. Your dog's comfort beats the perfect photo — every time, no exceptions.

Can't get to us? The salon comes to you.

The Wagtail van carries heated water, a full grooming table and a walk-up ramp for big or creaky dogs. Perfect for giant breeds, anxious travellers and busy households — call and we'll check when we're next in your village.
