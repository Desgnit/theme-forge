The academy

Twelve junior teams, boys' and girls' pathways from U7 to U18, and a genuine route to the first team. Trials every July — everyone gets a proper look.

How it works

Serious coaching, zero nonsense

Every age group trains once a week and plays Sunday football in the district leagues. Coaching follows the FA's player development framework — but the first rule of the academy is older than that: every kid plays every week, whatever the score.

Subs are £15 a month, and the hardship fund quietly covers anyone who needs it. Kit is provided, boots can be borrowed, and nobody has ever been turned away over money. That's a promise the committee has kept since 1974.

  • All coaches FA-qualified, DBS-checked and first-aid trained
  • Dedicated safeguarding officer at every session
  • Six current first-teamers came through these age groups

Questions

Parents ask us

What does a trial actually involve?

An hour of normal training and small-sided games. No clipboard line-ups, no cuts on the day — coaches watch, then we email everyone within a week.

What if my child doesn't get a place?

Nobody is sent away — squads flex before we ever say no, and our development group trains weekly with no match pressure until a league place opens.

What does it cost?

Trials are free. If your child joins, subs are £15 a month including kit and match fees — and the hardship fund exists so cost is never the reason a kid stops playing.

Who looks after safeguarding?

Sofia Kaur, our club safeguarding officer, oversees every age group. All coaches are DBS-checked, and our policies follow FA guidance — ask for a copy any time.

Coach with us

We fund FA coaching badges for volunteers who commit to a season. Fancy it? Talk to any age-group coach or drop us a line.
