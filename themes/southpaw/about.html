The club under the arches

One coach, one leaky railway arch, and fifteen years of Salford kids, shift workers and total beginners finding out what they're made of.

Our story

Started with a set of keys and a skip full of damp plaster

Marcus Delaney boxed out of clubs all over Manchester as an amateur — 38 bouts, a couple of finals, and a coach who once let him train for free for a year when things were rough at home. When his own boxing finished, that debt was the plan. In 2011 he took the keys to a leaky arch under the Frederick Road viaduct, dragged two bags and a ring in with borrowed hands, and opened the doors.

Fifteen years on, the arch is dry (mostly), the club is England Boxing affiliated, and hundreds of people train here every week. The rule hasn't changed since night one: everyone gets coached, nobody gets laughed at, and nobody is ever turned away over money.

What we're about

A proper club, not a fitness brand

Qualified & safeguarded

Every coach holds an England Boxing qualification, a current DBS check, safeguarding and first-aid training. Certificates are on the wall by the door — have a look while you wrap up.

Everyone gets coached

The beginner who's never made a fist gets the same eyes on them as the carded boxer with a bout in three weeks. If you're in the room, you're being coached. That's the deal.

Rooted in Salford

We run six-week non-contact blocks in local primary and secondary schools, take referrals from youth workers, and hold free juniors places for families who need them. The arch belongs to the postcode.

Want to help in the corner?

We're always after volunteers on club nights and members who fancy the England Boxing coaching pathway. No experience needed — call and talk to Marcus.
