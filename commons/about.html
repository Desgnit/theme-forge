Built by the village, run by the village

Nearly a century of dances, meetings, weddings, wakes, jumble sales and pantos — and still going strong, thanks to volunteers.

Our history

Raised by subscription in 1928

The hall was built in 1928 as a memorial to the men of Elmsworth who didn't come home from the Great War, paid for by village subscription and a legacy from the Ashwick estate. The Cotswold stone came from the quarry at Combe Norton; much of the labour came from the village itself.

Today it's a registered charity managed by a committee of volunteer trustees drawn from the village — a farmer, two retired teachers, the shop's owner and anyone else daft enough to say yes at the AGM. They look after the fabric of the building, set the hire rates, and organise the summer fete that keeps the roof fund topped up.

How the hall keeps running

The trustees

Nine villagers elected at the AGM each spring. They meet in the committee room on the second Tuesday of the month — every meeting is open, and minutes go on the noticeboard.

Volunteering

An hour on the fete tombola, a morning painting the porch, or a turn on the booking rota — every bit helps, and no one is ever asked twice. Get in touch and we'll find you a job you'll enjoy.

The annual fete

Held on the green every July since 1929, wet or fine. Dog show, produce tent, brass band and the famous duck race — it raises most of what keeps the hall's roof watertight.
