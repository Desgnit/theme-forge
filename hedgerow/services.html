Beyond the Shop

The shop is just the front room. The van rounds, the hampers, the counter orders and the café are how most people actually know us.

Veg box delivery rounds

The van goes out Tuesdays and Fridays through Bakewell, Baslow, Tideswell and the villages between. £18 a week, pause whenever you're away, and anything from the shop rides along free — milk, eggs, a joint for Sunday.

Hampers & corporate gifts

From a £25 thank-you box to a hundred client hampers at Christmas, all packed here with a hand-written card. Tell us the budget and who it's for; we'll do the choosing. Order corporate hampers by late November — we mean it.

Butchery counter orders

Ring ahead for anything the counter does: a rib on the bone for eight, a crown of lamb, sausages by the tray for a barbecue. Give Tom two days' notice for the fancy stuff, an hour for most things.

The café corner

Eight tables by the wood burner, cooking what the shop sells: our sausages, our eggs, Friday's bread. Breakfast until 11:30, soup and sandwiches after, cake all day. The kitchen closes at 3 so the pans are clean by milking.

Whole & half lamb for the freezer

Our own Lleyn lamb, jointed to your instructions — chops or steaks, legs whole or halved, shoulders rolled or diced. A half lamb runs about £70 and fills two freezer drawers. Spoken for fast in autumn.

School & group visits

Spring term brings the lambing shed tours; autumn brings apple pressing. Schools, WIs and walking groups welcome by arrangement — wellies essential, and everyone leaves knowing where their dinner comes from.

How a veg box round works

From the field to your doorstep in a morning

  1. 1

    Pick your size

    Small feeds two, standard feeds four. Tell us the things you genuinely won't eat and we'll swap around them.

  2. 2

    Find your round

    Tuesdays go north of the river, Fridays go south. Ring the shop and we'll tell you which day passes your door.

  3. 3

    We pick that morning

    Boxes are packed from the field and the walled garden the same morning they're delivered — never the night before.

  4. 4

    On the doorstep by one

    Left where you've told us, with a note of what's in it and what to eat first. Empty boxes come back on the next round.

Start with a single box

No standing order, no commitment — try one veg box and see what a Peak District week tastes like. Worst case, you eat very well once.
