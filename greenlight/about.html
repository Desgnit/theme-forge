The instructor in the passenger seat

One instructor, two cars, twelve years of Leicester roundabouts — and a simple rule that has never once been broken: no shouting, ever.

Marcus's story

From the depot office to the passenger seat

Marcus Bell spent his twenties managing a parcel depot and his weekends teaching his younger sister to drive after two instructors had reduced her to tears. She passed first time — and he realised the teaching mattered more to him than the depot ever had. He retrained, qualified as a DVSA Approved Driving Instructor, and has now spent 12 years teaching Leicester learners full time, earning Grade A — the top grade — at every standards check since.

The philosophy hasn't changed since that first lesson with his sister: people learn when they feel safe. Mistakes are information, not failures. Stall it five times and you'll hear "no problem, again" five times. That calm is why nervous learners are the biggest source of referrals — and why so many of the photos on the pass wall say "first time".

How Marcus teaches

Calm, structured, on purpose

No shouting, ever

Raised voices teach fear, not driving. Every mistake gets the same response: pull over somewhere safe, talk it through, try again. That's the whole method, and it's non-negotiable.

Built for nervous learners

Anxious, neurodivergent, or shaken by a bad experience elsewhere — lessons move at your pace, with dual controls always there so nothing can really go wrong.

Two cars, your choice

A manual Ford Fiesta and an automatic Toyota Yaris hybrid — both small, light and easy to place, both dual-controlled, serviced monthly and cleaned before every lesson.
