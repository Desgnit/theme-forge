Stop 01 · Bangkok · 12 to 14 Feb · 3 nights

Three days in the city that never slows down

We land at breakfast time with a whole city to eat. Bangkok is gold leaf and diesel, incense and chilli smoke, seven million scooters and the best street food on the planet at £1 to £2 a plate.

  • Yaowarat Road food crawl. Chinatown after dark: neon, lanterns, charcoal woks. We graze stall to stall and eat like royalty for the price of one Manchester takeaway.
  • Dawn longtail through the Thonburi canals. Old Bangkok on stilts, monks collecting alms, the city waking up around us.
  • The Grand Palace and Wat Pho. A 46 metre reclining Buddha covered in gold. Polly will not believe the size of its feet.
  • Ayutthaya by train. Third class tickets, windows open. Then bicycles through a ruined 400 year old royal capital, temples wrapped in tree roots.
  • Optional detour: Lopburi, the monkey city. A town run by macaques. The boys will never stop talking about it.
Where we sleep Family rooms in Silom or Sukhumvit, close to the Skytrain: £45 to £65 a night for all five of us.
Wow moment

The fish bigger than Polly

Bungsamran Lake is home to Mekong giant catfish over 50 kilos. Around £30 a peg with tackle and a guide included. When one takes the bait it will take two of us on the rod, twenty minutes of shaking arms, and one photograph that gets framed. Polly weighs less than this fish.

Stop 02 · Khao Sok · 14 to 17 Feb · 3 nights

The night we sleep on the lake

A short flight south (Bangkok to Surat Thani, about £40 each) and the world changes completely: Khao Sok is rainforest older than the Amazon, limestone towers wrapped in jungle, and a flooded emerald valley called Cheow Lan Lake.

  • Khao Sok Elephant Sanctuary. A half day feeding and walking with rescued elephants. Ethical, no riding, hands on. £35 to £45 each and worth every penny of it.
  • Overnight in a floating raft house. 500 Rai Floating Resort, out on the lake itself. Wooden cabins on pontoons, limestone karsts straight out of emerald water, and you swim right off your own porch. £60 to £90 a night for all five.
  • Dawn wildlife safari by longtail. Gibbons, hornbills, langurs, macaques, and if the lake is kind, wild elephants at the waterline.
  • Freshwater fishing for snakehead. Aggressive, hard fighting, spectacular. £40 to £60 for a half day.
  • Jungle trek under 300 metre cliffs, through forest that was already ancient when the dinosaurs went.
Wow moment

Waking up on the water

Mist on the lake. Limestone towers on every side, glowing pink in the first light. Gibbons singing the valley awake, the strangest and most beautiful sound in Asia. You roll out of bed, take three steps, and dive into warm emerald water before breakfast. No wifi. Nobody will mind.

Stop 03 · Koh Tao · 17 to 22 Feb · 5 nights

The island where the boys become divers

Ferry across from Surat Thani (about £20 each) to Turtle Island: the cheapest, friendliest place on Earth to learn to dive. Warm 29 degree water, 20 metre visibility, and dive schools that have trained half the planet.

  • PADI Open Water for the boys. Three to four days of pool drills and real ocean dives, about £250 each. A qualification that lasts a lifetime, earned at 14 and 15.
  • Polly gets her moment too. Bubblemaker or Discover Scuba in the shallows, about £40, with an instructor all to herself.
  • Shark Bay snorkelling. Blacktip reef sharks cruising waist deep water. Completely harmless, completely unforgettable.
  • Sail Rock. The Gulf's best dive site. From February, whale sharks, the biggest fish in the sea, pass through.
  • Private longtail charter for a day around the island's coves, £80 to £120. Plus a deep sea fishing run: barracuda, trevally, Spanish mackerel.
  • Sunset hike to John-Suwan viewpoint, where two bays meet in a ribbon of sand.
Where we sleep Dive school lodges from £10 to £20 a night (discounted with the courses), or a Sairee Beach guesthouse at £30 to £50.
Wow moment

Their first breath underwater

Somewhere around day three, ten metres down over a coral garden, the boys will look at each other and realise they are actually doing this. Certified divers before they can drive. And they will always remember it was this trip, and this family, that made it happen.

Stop 04 · Koh Samui · 22 to 25 Feb · 3 nights

Pigs, palms and one last emerald lagoon

One easy ferry hop (about £15 each) to the soft landing: a family bungalow with a pool in Lamai or Maenam, £50 to £80 a night, and three days of the greatest hits before we fly home.

  • Pig Island. A longtail out to Koh Madsum, where the pigs paddle out to meet the boat, then snorkelling off Koh Tan. About £30 each. Polly may refuse to leave.
  • Ang Thong Marine Park. Forty two jungle islands rising out of turquoise sea. We kayak between the karsts and climb to the emerald lagoon, a sealed lake of green glass hidden inside an island.
  • Na Muang waterfalls. Purple granite, jungle pools, one last freshwater swim.
  • The wind down. Pool, beach, massages for Mum, mango sticky rice for everyone.
  • The smooth exit. Fly Samui to Bangkok on the 25th, connecting neatly to the 19:15 flight home. No mad dashes.
Wow moment

Pigs that swim out to say hello

The boat noses into the shallows off Koh Madsum and the welcome committee arrives: actual pigs, actually swimming, snouts up like little pink tugboats. It is the single silliest, happiest thing in the Gulf of Thailand, and it is ours for about £30 a head.
