The bloke behind the buffer

One detailer, two vans, and a mild-to-serious obsession with swirl marks. This is Showroom Mobile Valeting.

Our story

It started with one swirl mark

Jamie detailed his own car every Sunday for years before anyone paid him to do it. The turning point was a drive-through hand wash that returned his pride and joy covered in swirl marks he could see from the kitchen window. He bought a machine polisher to fix them, fell down the detailing rabbit hole, and never climbed back out. Showroom went full-time in 2018 with one van and a pressure washer.

Today there are two fully self-contained vans — 400 litres of water, power and lighting in each — and a second detailer, trained by Jamie and signed off on Jamie's own car. But every booking still gets the same test: if it wouldn't pass under his garage inspection lights, it isn't finished. On products, the philosophy is simple: pH-neutral pre-washes, dedicated iron removers, ceramic sealants — trade-quality gear chosen because it works, never because a label pays us to carry it.

Why people rebook

Fussy, and proud of it

Swirl marks are personal

Every wash is two buckets, grit guards and fresh microfibre — nothing that touched a wheel ever touches paint. We're the reason your car won't need us as often, and we're fine with that.

The vans carry everything

Water tanks, on-board power, lighting, extraction and every product we use. Your outside tap and extension lead stay exactly where they are — we just need somewhere to park.

Honest about your paint

Paint depth is measured before any machine work, and if a correction isn't safe or isn't worth it, we'll say so and suggest the cheaper option instead. That's why customers stay.
